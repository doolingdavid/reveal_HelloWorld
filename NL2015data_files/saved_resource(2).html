These Sweet Baseball Facts Will Have You Rounding the Bases

Did you know?
With a move to LA just months away in 1957, the Brooklyn Dodgers decided to trade their entire Minor League team to the Chicago Cubs in exchange for theirs.
Did you know?
Atlanta Braves great Chipper Jones named his son Shea due to his amazing success at the New York Mets’ Shea Stadium throughout his career.
Did you know?
The Brooklyn Nets were first known as the Americans, but switched in 1968 to rhyme with New York’s other sports teams, the Mets and Jets.
Did you know?
Just in case they split their pants bending over to clean the home plate, MLB umpires are officially required to wear black underwear.
Did you know?
A Yale study found that from the release of a 90 mph pitch, a batter has 400 milliseconds to judge it and swing. If the swing is 7 milliseconds too early or late, it will go foul.
Did you know?
Baseball mascots have become so popular among fan culture that a Mascot Hall of Fame was created in Whiting, Indiana. The first inductee was the Phillie Phanatic.
